Fukushima Reactor 2

On March 11th 2011, a devastating earthquake and a tsunami caused by it struck Japan. Tens of thousands lost their lives that day, tens of thousands now miss a loved one.

Among the consequences of the disaster there was unprecedented damage to the nuclear power plant Fukushima Daiichi (Fukushima 1). The ruin is leaking poison right at the moment and will not stop doing so for years. Nobody ever cleaned up a radioactivity spill of that size and probably nobody even can do so. I do not think any of my contemporarys will live long enough to see a resolution of this crisis.

To heighten awareness of the fact that the accident in Fukushima is not over, not even contained, that the fire is not put out, I will here republish data of thermometers in reactor 2. Reactor 2 is interesting because it's thermometers are the only ones which do not show it's contents cooling down.

The Media are blaming this on an instrument failure. Instrument failure, however, is not bound to happen on several instruments at onces, and thermocouple failures usually do not look much like this.

Everybody is bound to make their own conclusions. The original Data in Text Form is here: Tepco Source Temperature Data Block 2 - csv.

Fukushima Daiichi Reactor 2 Temperatures in February 2012 Image Link

Note the difference between Temperature in "CRD Housing Upper Part" and "Reactor Pressure Vessel", two thermometers mounted not far apart from each other.

Note further, how Tepco and the media focus attention on the PRV Thermometer which shows quite surprising jumps, and which now again is said to represent the Situation in the whole Reactor, despite its measurements were said to be faulty before.
